# Business Utilization Rubric — 30 Points

| Item | Max | Full Credit | Partial Credit | Deduction Triggers | Required Evidence |
|---|---:|---|---|---|---|
| Business plan alignment | 6 | explicit business plan, target customer, timing, and execution owner | strategic fit but weak execution details | no business plan or only retrospective assertion | business plan, project plan, customer proposal |
| Samsung SDS product/roadmap fit | 6 | directly mapped to SDS product, technology, or roadmap item | related area but product/timing unclear | no roadmap/product evidence | SDS product materials, roadmap, architecture strategy |
| Market/customer problem clarity | 5 | concrete customer pain point and market demand evidence | general market need only | marketability asserted without source | VOC, market report, trend report, sales material |
| Competitive utilization advantage | 5 | competitor comparison shows advantage in function, cost, performance, operations, or IP position | comparison exists but dimension is limited | no competitor comparison or unclear advantage | competitor matrix, benchmark, external analysis |
| Commercialization feasibility | 4 | deployment path, owner, schedule, resources, and risks are concrete | plausible but missing owner/schedule/resources | no execution path | product plan, PoC, customer project, delivery plan |
| Reuse/scalability | 4 | reusable across products/customers/industries with evidence | plausible scalability but weak proof | single-customer or one-off usage | platform docs, reusable module docs, roadmap |

## Scoring Bands
- 27–30: Exceptional business evidence, including plan, SDS roadmap, and competitor support.
- 23–26: Strong business fit with some evidence gaps.
- 18–22: Plausible utilization but limited objective support.
- 12–17: Narrow or weakly supported business relevance.
- 0–11: Insufficient business basis.
