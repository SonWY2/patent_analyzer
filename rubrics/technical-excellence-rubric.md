# Technical Excellence Rubric — 30 Points

| Item | Max | Full Credit | Partial Credit | Deduction Triggers | Required Evidence |
|---|---:|---|---|---|---|
| Technical novelty / differentiation | 8 | Clear structural or methodological distinction over prior art, competitors, or papers | Differentiation exists but scope/effect is partly unclear | Ordinary application of known technique; only inventor assertion | attorney review, prior art, paper, competitor comparison, trend report |
| Problem-solution-effect logic | 6 | Specific problem, technical means, and effect are logically connected | Some links are plausible but underspecified | abstract problem or effect not tied to means | disclosure, architecture, examples, test results |
| Implementation specificity | 6 | Components, data flow, algorithms, interfaces, and operating conditions are concrete | direction is clear but key modules are abstract | idea-only or missing implementation path | architecture, PoC, source excerpt, design document |
| Effect verifiability | 5 | effects are measurable and supported by data or credible comparison | qualitative effect only or weak measurement | vague, non-measurable, or contradicted effect | benchmark, experiment, customer case, paper, external evaluation |
| Claim expandability / defensibility | 5 | multiple embodiments and defensible claim scope are supported | limited fallback embodiments | narrow implementation, easy design-around, high prior-art overlap | attorney review, claim draft, prior-art map, verified variants |

## Scoring Bands
- 27–30: Exceptional; objective evidence supports high confidence and max-score gate may pass.
- 23–26: Strong; some evidence or cap gaps remain.
- 18–22: Meaningful but needs stronger novelty, specificity, or effect evidence.
- 12–17: Limited; implementation or patentable distinction is weak.
- 0–11: Insufficient technical basis.

## Mandatory Notes
Creative hypotheses may improve research direction but cannot raise the score unless verified in the evidence pack and accepted by Evidence Auditor.
